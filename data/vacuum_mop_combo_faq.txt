# Vacuum-Mop Combo Robot FAQ

## Vacuum And Mop Workflow
1. Vacuum only: turn off mopping in the app and keep suction mode enabled.
2. Vacuum first, mop second: choose a vacuum-then-mop workflow and set suction, water output, and room order.
3. Simultaneous vacuuming and mopping: use this for normal dust, but vacuum first for crumbs, hair, or heavy debris.
4. Mop only: install the water tank and mop pad, clean the dustbin, and choose mop-only mode.
5. Suction during mopping: some models reduce suction slightly; use high suction when stronger pickup is needed.
6. Room-specific settings: set suction and water output separately for kitchens, bathrooms, carpets, tile, and hardwood.
7. Dry and wet mopping: dry mop removes dust and hair; wet mop removes light stains.
8. Auto-wash disabled: turn off mop washing if you only want vacuuming.
9. Mop-lift behavior: supported models lift the mop when docking, crossing carpets, or finishing cleaning.
10. Repeat cleaning: set 1-3 passes for sticky or high-traffic areas.

## Water Tank And Mop System
11. Tank leaks after filling: check the cap, gasket, outlet tube, and fill level.
12. Too much or too little water: adjust water output in the app; use low for hardwood and medium/high for tile.
13. Empty tank detection: supported models pause mopping or notify the app when water is low.
14. Cleaning solution: dilute robot-safe cleaner according to product instructions; never add concentrated cleaner directly.
15. Electric mop does not rotate: remove hair, check the mop mount, restart, and contact service if the motor still does not turn.
16. Mop pad smells after washing: soak in warm water with mild detergent, rinse, dry fully, and replace if odor remains.
17. Disposable mop installation: attach flat to the mop bracket, lock the edges, and discard after use.
18. Washable mop frequency: wash after every mopping run.
19. Streaky water marks: clean or replace hardened mop pads and lower water output.
20. Edges are not mopped well: enable edge-mop enhancement if available or use spot mopping.
21. Dirty-water tank full: empty and rinse the tank and filter immediately.
22. Dock cannot wash mop: check clean-water level, pump intake, hoses, and station sensors.
23. Loose mop bracket: inspect clips and press the bracket into place until it clicks.
24. Repeated wiping in one area: check for a detected stain or sensor misread, then set a no-mop area if needed.
25. Long-term tank storage: empty the tank, dry it, and store it with the seal protected.

## Cleaning Optimization
26. Greasy kitchen floor: vacuum first, add diluted robot-safe cleaner, and run multiple mop passes.
27. Pet urine: absorb liquid manually first, then use pet-safe cleaner and spot mopping.
28. Gum or glue: remove manually before using the robot.
29. Carpet areas: create no-mop zones or enable mop lift.
30. Large homes: use resume-after-refill if supported or divide the map into zones.
31. Hardwood warping: stop wet mopping, dry the floor, ventilate, and use low-water or dry-mop mode later.
32. Tile grout stains: use high-frequency vibration if supported and repeat mopping with tile-safe cleaner.
33. Fine dust after mopping: clean filters and brush first, then vacuum before mopping.
34. Outdoor balcony: avoid open outdoor use; use only enclosed, dry balconies.
35. Children's room spills: use mild cleaner, spot mop, and follow with clean-water mopping.
36. Carpet edge water: create a buffer zone around carpets and enable mop lift.
37. Old concrete dust: vacuum twice with high suction before mopping.
38. Mirror furniture collisions: add no-go or slow-down zones near reflective furniture.
39. Robot pushes debris while mopping: clear suction inlet, increase suction, and manually remove large debris.
40. Under-bed cleaning: clear cables and clutter, then increase suction and mop passes for that zone.

## Hardware Alarms
41. Mopping-system alarm: reseat the tank and mop, remove debris, and restart.
42. Tank not detected: clean the tank sensor, reinstall firmly, and check the latch.
43. Clicking from mop rotation: remove wrapped hair and small debris, then lubricate approved bearings lightly.
44. Cleaning-station water-level alarm: refill clean water, empty dirty water, clean sensors, and restart.
45. Robot stops while mopping: check battery, blocked mop module, and sensors.
46. Mop bracket not installed: insert the bracket fully or replace broken clips.
47. Clogged outlet tube: gently clear the tube with a toothpick or thin soft wire and rinse the tank.
48. Robot shakes during mopping: flatten the mop pad, clean drive wheels, and check for standing water.
49. Mop lift fails: enable the feature, clean the lift mechanism, and contact support if the motor is stuck.
50. Cleaner concentration alarm: empty and rinse the tank, then refill with correctly diluted solution.
51. Black scratches while mopping: check for grit trapped in the mop or a worn bracket.
52. Mop module runs while charging: restart the robot and reset settings if the issue continues.
53. Dirty-water filter clogged: rinse, dry, and reinstall the filter.
54. Mop motor stuck and hot: power off immediately and contact service.
55. Mopping turns off after threshold crossing: verify threshold height and enable resume mopping after obstacle crossing if supported.

## Consumables
56. Mop pad life: replace washable pads every 3-6 months, or every 1-2 months for heavy oil or pet homes.
57. Tank gasket life: replace every 6-12 months or immediately if cracked or leaking.
58. Cleaning brush in station: remove hair after each dirty-water dump and rinse weekly.
59. Mop bearings: add 1-2 drops of approved lubricant every 3 months if the manual allows it.
60. Mopping filters: do not mix vacuum filters and dirty-water filters.
61. Sensor cleaning: use a dry lint-free cloth and avoid alcohol or wet wipes.
62. Auto-empty plus mop-wash consumables: replace bags every 1-2 months, mop pads about every 3 months, and filters every 6 months.
63. Broken bracket clip: replace the original bracket rather than gluing it.
64. Station water pump: flush with clean water after long storage and descale every 3 months if water is hard.
65. Disposable mops: do not wash and reuse them.

## Scenario Tips
66. Humid season: use dry-mop mode, reduce water output, and run a dehumidifier.
67. Heated floors: use low water output and ventilate after mopping to avoid visible water marks.
68. No balcony for drying pads: towel-dry and place in a ventilated indoor area or use cool air.
69. Homes with infants: use clean water only and ensure the floor is fully dry before crawling.
70. Multi-floor homes: create one map per floor and carry the robot manually.
71. Laundry-area water: create a no-mop zone around washers or floor drains.
72. Heavy pet shedding: vacuum first with high suction, then mop.
73. Travel mode: schedule cleaning only if water, dustbin, dock, and consumables are ready.
74. Post-renovation cleaning: do not use the robot for construction dust, cement chunks, or sharp debris.
75. Snack spills: use spot vacuum-mop with high suction and medium water.
76. Small toys and slippers: enable advanced obstacle avoidance and clear the floor before scheduled cleaning.
77. Bathroom mats: set no-mop zones to prevent mold and trapped moisture.
78. Sticky summer spills: use spot mopping quickly before stains dry.
79. Study room with rugs: no-mop the rug and spot clean around the desk.
80. Dusty neighborhoods: keep windows closed and use fewer but deeper cleaning cycles.

## Software And Service
81. Save and delete maps in map management after a complete mapping run.
82. Share settings with family through device sharing or QR invite.
83. Review and delete cleaning records in the app's cleaning-history page.
84. Schedule mopping with room, time, suction, water output, and repeat frequency.
85. Disable unnecessary push notifications such as low water or cleaning complete.
86. Firmware updates usually preserve settings, but factory reset may be needed after abnormal behavior.
87. Custom route cleaning is useful for high-priority paths and repeated spill zones.
88. The robot can run scheduled tasks even when the phone is offline if Wi-Fi and tasks are already configured.
89. Restore default cleaning parameters in app cleaning settings.
90. Consumable life is usually available in the app's consumables page.
91. Warranty usually covers non-consumable mop-module parts but not pads or seals.
92. Out-of-warranty cost depends on pump, motor, sensor, or bracket replacement.
93. Old models cannot add mop-lift through software if the hardware is missing.
94. Mainboard replacement usually requires Wi-Fi setup, remapping, and parameter setup again.
95. Accessory model numbers are listed in the manual, app store, or product label.
96. Third-party mop pads are risky if size, thickness, or absorption differs.
97. After one year of weaker mopping, replace mop pads, filters, and worn brushes before replacing motors.
98. After moving, inspect the body, mop module, filters, sensors, and maps.
99. Official deep maintenance may include cleaning, consumable replacement, and calibration.
100. Consider replacing the robot after 3+ years if suction, mopping, mapping, and repairs are all poor and repair cost exceeds about one-third of a new model.
