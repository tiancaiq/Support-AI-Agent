# Robot Vacuum And Vacuum-Mop Maintenance Guide

## Daily Maintenance
1. Wipe the robot shell with a soft dry cloth after use.
2. Remove hair and threads from the bumper gap after each run.
3. Check drive wheels and caster wheel daily for hair, string, or grit.
4. Avoid collisions with sharp furniture or metal stands.
5. Wipe robot and dock charging contacts daily.
6. Keep hands away from rotating brushes while the robot is running.
7. Wipe cliff and edge sensors with a dry cloth.
8. Disconnect power before cleaning the robot body.
9. Clean dustbin latches so the bin seats correctly.
10. Return the robot to the dock after each use.
11. Check screws and visible fasteners weekly.
12. Brush dust away from cooling vents weekly.
13. Press the bumper weekly to confirm normal rebound.
14. Wipe buttons and indicator lights weekly.
15. Lubricate wheel axles monthly only if the manual allows it.
16. Inspect removable batteries monthly for swelling or leakage.
17. Clean obstacle cameras and sensors monthly.
18. Do a full exterior dusting monthly.
19. Stop the robot if new abnormal noise appears.
20. Confirm every replacement part matches the model.

## Vacuum System Maintenance
21. Remove hair from the main brush after each run.
22. Clean side brushes daily in pet homes.
23. Clean the brush chamber weekly.
24. Replace worn brush strips before cleaning performance drops.
25. Clear air channels weekly.
26. Check auto-empty channels daily for clogs.
27. Wipe the dustbin interior after emptying it.
28. Always install the filter before running the robot.
29. If suction drops, inspect the dustbin, filter, brush, and air channel first.
30. Clean brushless suction inlets weekly if the model has no main brush.
31. Confirm auto-empty bags are seated and sealed.
32. Clean carpet fibers from the brush after carpet runs.
33. Inspect main-brush motor area monthly for heat or noise.
34. Protect brush bearings from water and dust.
35. Replace side brushes when bristles are short or bent.
36. Clean dust sensors weekly.
37. Clear the auto-empty station outlet weekly.
38. Remove sand and grit after cleaning rough floors.
39. Empty dustbin fully after each run in high-dust homes.
40. Check suction-level switching monthly.

## Mopping System Maintenance
41. Wash mop pads after every mopping run.
42. Dry mop pads in a ventilated area to prevent mildew.
43. Clean mop brackets after each use.
44. Use clean water in the tank and avoid debris or scale.
45. Empty remaining water after mopping.
46. Descale the water tank monthly if water is hard.
47. Empty and rinse dirty-water tanks after every auto-wash cycle.
48. Clean station washing brushes daily for auto-wash models.
49. Lubricate mop-bracket shafts only if supported by the manual.
50. Install only clean, soft mop pads.
51. Wipe tank gaskets weekly and inspect for cracks.
52. Clean water-level sensors weekly.
53. Clear outlet tubes immediately if water output becomes abnormal.
54. Check mop motors for heat or noise.
55. Remove mop odor with mild detergent, rinse, and dry fully.
56. Clean the robot's tank connector before installing the tank.
57. Inspect mop-lift modules daily on supported models.
58. Discard disposable mop pads after one use.
59. Wipe the bottom mopping module weekly.
60. Descale station pump lines monthly if scale appears.

## Consumable Replacement
61. Main brush: replace every 3-6 months; sooner for pets or heavy hair.
62. Side brush: replace every 2-3 months or when deformed.
63. HEPA filter: clean monthly and replace every 3-6 months.
64. Dust bag: replace every 1-2 months; sooner for pet homes.
65. Washable mop pad: replace every 1-3 months depending on stains and odor.
66. Disposable mop pad: replace every run.
67. Rubber brush: replace when strips crack, harden, or deform.
68. Water-tank gasket: replace every 6-12 months or when leaking.
69. Dirty-water filter: rinse weekly and replace every 6 months.
70. Cleaning-station brush: replace when bristles deform or shed.
71. Drive-wheel tread: replace when slipping continues after cleaning.
72. Caster wheel: replace if it no longer spins smoothly.
73. Battery: replace when runtime becomes much shorter or the battery swells.
74. Dock dust bag seal: replace if dust leaks.
75. Charging contacts: service or replace if oxidation cannot be cleaned.

## Environment Care
76. Keep the dock on a flat, dry, ventilated surface.
77. Keep the dock area free of cables and clutter.
78. Avoid direct sunlight on the robot and dock.
79. Avoid high heat above 40 C and freezing temperatures below 0 C.
80. Avoid corrosive liquids such as alcohol, bleach, disinfectant, or dish soap.
81. Dry the robot immediately if it touches wet floors.
82. Do not run the robot through standing water.
83. Protect stairs with physical barriers if needed.
84. Remove stones, bottle caps, cables, socks, and toys before scheduled cleaning.
85. In pet homes, clean the brush and dustbin after every run.
86. In humid weather, dry the robot and dock after cleaning.
87. In dusty homes, clean filters and sensors more often.
88. In homes with long hair, use anti-tangle brushes and clean brush ends often.
89. For hardwood, use low water output and ventilate after mopping.
90. For carpets, avoid wet mopping unless the model has reliable mop lift.

## Long-Term Storage
91. Charge the battery to about 50-70% before long storage.
92. Store the robot in a cool, dry place.
93. Recharge every 1-2 months during storage.
94. Empty dustbin and water tanks before storage.
95. Wash and fully dry mop pads before storage.
96. Clean brushes, wheels, sensors, and charging contacts.
97. Keep the dock unplugged if unused for a long time.
98. Store consumables in sealed dry bags if possible.
99. Check for battery swelling before reuse.
100. Run a short test cycle after long storage.

## Preventive Checks
101. Run a no-load test monthly and listen for noise.
102. Check that sensors are not scratched or fogged.
103. Confirm maps and no-go zones after furniture changes.
104. Review app consumable-life reminders monthly.
105. Update firmware only when the robot is charged and online.
106. Confirm scheduled tasks after firmware updates.
107. Test auto-return after moving the dock.
108. Test spot cleaning after remapping.
109. Clean dock infrared and charging areas weekly.
110. Replace damaged shells or cracked brackets before they affect sensors.
111. Do not paste strong adhesive stickers on the robot shell.
112. Carry the robot gently; do not drag or drop it.
113. Clean pet accidents immediately and disinfect safely.
114. Keep flammable items away while charging.
115. If water enters the body, power off and contact service.
116. If a burning smell appears, stop use immediately.
117. If repeated alarms happen after cleaning, record the code and contact support.
118. Use official accessories for parts that affect sealing, charging, or movement.
119. Keep a small maintenance kit: soft cloth, brush, tweezers, scissors, and approved lubricant.
120. Follow a simple routine: empty, wipe, untangle, dry, dock.
