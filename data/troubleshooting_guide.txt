# Robot Vacuum And Vacuum-Mop Troubleshooting Guide

## Power, Charging, And Battery
1. No response on power-on: check adapter, cable, outlet, charging dock power, and battery seating; contact service if the mainboard may be faulty.
2. Shuts down immediately: battery may be empty, overheated, or swollen; fully charge, cool the robot, and replace swollen batteries.
3. Will not charge: wipe metal charging contacts, test the adapter, reseat the robot on the dock, and replace a failed battery if needed.
4. Charging light flashes abnormally: inspect oxidation, dust, adapter faults, and battery health.
5. Battery life drops sharply: avoid over-discharge, calibrate with a full cycle, and replace the battery if runtime is below about half of normal.
6. Powers off after full charge: inspect battery swelling and power-management board faults.
7. Severe heat or burning smell: power off immediately, clear air channels, and contact service for motor or battery inspection.
8. Low-temperature startup failure: bring the robot to room temperature above 5 C, wait 30 minutes, then recharge.
9. Hot while charging: improve ventilation, pause charging until cool, and contact service if overheating repeats.
10. Sparks while charging: stop charging, clean contacts, replace damaged adapters, and inspect the dock.

## Connectivity And App
11. Cannot connect to Wi-Fi: use 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi, confirm password, restart router and robot, and reset robot Wi-Fi.
12. App cannot control robot: check network, app version, robot online status, and device binding.
13. Delayed notifications: enable app notifications, check network, and reconnect the robot.
14. Cleaning history missing: confirm cloud sync, update the app, and restart the robot.
15. App crashes: close background processes, update, clear phone memory, or reinstall.
16. New phone cannot connect: unbind from old phone if required, log in, and add the device again.
17. Password forgotten: use the app password-reset flow.
18. Multiple phones: use device sharing or same-account login depending on brand rules.
19. Wi-Fi module alarm: restart router, reset Wi-Fi, reconnect, and contact service if the module fails.
20. Firmware update fails: keep robot charged and online, retry near router, and avoid power loss.

## Navigation, Mapping, And Docking
21. Map is incomplete: remove mirrors, reflective tile glare, and moving objects, then remap.
22. Map is distorted after moving robot manually: recalibrate positioning or rebuild the map.
23. Route is chaotic: enable planned cleaning, clear movable obstacles, and remap.
24. Robot cannot find dock: clear the dock area, wipe recharge sensors, and place dock against a wall.
25. Auto-return fails after cleaning: verify dock location on the map and sensor cleanliness.
26. Repeatedly pauses during cleaning: empty dustbin, clean filter, charge battery, and wipe sensors.
27. Missed rooms: check closed doors, no-go zones, and incomplete map boundaries.
28. Enters no-go zones: redraw boundaries and save the map again.
29. Cannot select room cleaning: rebuild the map and update app/firmware.
30. Multi-floor map switching fails: build separate maps for each floor and place robot correctly before starting.
31. Threshold crossing fails: check height rating, remove debris, and guide the robot once if needed.
32. Stuck under furniture: measure clearance and set no-go zones under low furniture.
33. Mirror or glass causes mapping errors: create no-go zones or cover reflective surfaces during mapping.
34. Newly added furniture is hit: recalibrate obstacle sensors and refresh the map.
35. Robot loses map after restart: check storage or firmware issues and remap.

## Sensors And Obstacle Avoidance
36. Frequent furniture collisions: clean front sensors/camera and inspect bumper rebound.
37. Bumper sensor alarm: remove trapped debris and press bumper to test rebound.
38. Cliff detection failure: wipe bottom sensors and test on a flat surface away from stairs.
39. Sensor abnormal alarm: clean all front, side, and bottom sensors with a dry cloth.
40. Edge-cleaning drifts from wall: clean edge sensors and inspect side-brush wear.
41. Obstacle avoidance gets worse: clean sensors, enable advanced obstacle mode, and remap.
42. Robot falls-risk near stairs: set a virtual wall or physical barrier even if cliff sensors exist.
43. Light-colored or reflective furniture misread: add no-go zones or cover reflective parts.
44. Sensor blocked by dust or water: dry and wipe before restarting.
45. Camera-based models fail in strong light: reduce glare or remap under normal lighting.

## Suction, Brushes, And Dustbin
46. Suction drops: clean dustbin, filter, suction inlet, air duct, main brush, and side brush.
47. Main brush does not rotate: remove hair and debris, reinstall, and inspect motor resistance.
48. Side brush does not rotate: clean the shaft, check installation, and replace failed motor parts.
49. Robot pushes debris instead of vacuuming: clear suction inlet and increase suction.
50. Large debris gets stuck: manually remove oversized debris and clean the brush chamber.
51. Hair is left behind: clean brush ends, use anti-tangle brush, and increase suction.
52. Filter-clogged alarm persists: replace damaged filter and wipe the filter sensor.
53. Dustbin-not-installed alarm: reseat dustbin and clean the latch/sensor.
54. Dustbin leaks dust: clean gasket and latch, then replace worn seals.
55. Dustbin odor: empty frequently, wash if allowed, dry fully, and replace odor-trapped filters.
56. Main brush clicking: remove hard debris, check bearings, and replace worn rubber strips.
57. High noise: remove brush/side-brush tangles, lubricate wheel modules if allowed, and inspect motor.
58. Wheel slipping: clean hair and dust from drive wheels and replace worn tread.
59. Caster wheel stuck: remove hair, clean axle, and apply a small amount of approved lubricant.
60. Edge cleaning poor: replace worn side brushes and enable edge-cleaning mode.

## Mopping And Water System
61. Water tank leaks: tighten cap, check seals, clear outlet tubes, and replace cracked tank.
62. No water output: refill tank, raise water level setting, and clear outlet with a toothpick.
63. Too much water: lower water output and inspect valve or gasket damage.
64. Electric mop does not rotate: remove hair, reseat mop bracket, and inspect motor.
65. Auto-wash mop fails: check clean water, dirty-water tank, washing brush, pump, and station sensors.
66. Mop pad falls off: clean or replace Velcro/clips and install the pad flat.
67. Mop-lift fails: clean lifting module and verify feature is enabled.
68. Dirty-water filter clogged: rinse, dry, reinstall, and clean regularly.
69. Water marks remain: clean or replace mop pad, reduce water, and slow the robot.
70. Sticky stains remain: manually pre-treat heavy stains and run spot mopping.
71. Cleaner residue: reduce cleaning solution concentration and run clean-water mopping.
72. Mop smell: wash with mild detergent, dry fully, and replace if odor persists.
73. Black scratches while mopping: check for grit in mop pad or worn bracket.
74. Standing water near bathroom/laundry: set no-mop zones and dry the floor before use.
75. Mopping module stays active while charging: restart and reset settings if needed.

## Auto-Empty And Dock Station
76. Auto-empty fails: check dust bag, station cover, dust channel, and robot docking alignment.
77. Auto-empty suction weak: replace full bag and clean station air path.
78. Auto-empty alarm after bag change: reseat the bag and check bag sensor.
79. Dock indicator off: check outlet, adapter, and dock power input.
80. Dock moves when robot returns: place dock on a flat wall-backed surface with anti-slip base.
81. Dock smells: replace bag, clean dust path, and keep station dry.
82. Mop-wash station does not drain: clean drain filter and dirty-water path.
83. Clean-water station does not feed water: check tank seating, inlet, and pump.
84. Dock infrared area dirty: wipe with dry cloth.
85. Station maintenance: clean weekly in pet or high-dust homes.

## Environment And Scenario Issues
86. Long-pile carpet blocks movement: use high suction only if supported, or set no-go zones.
87. Hardwood warps after mopping: dry immediately and use minimum water later.
88. Tile grout remains dirty: run multiple passes or manually deep clean grout.
89. Pet litter clogs brush: use high suction for light litter and manually remove large clumps.
90. Pet hair tangles: clean brush after every run and use pet-specific brush/filter.
91. Kitchen oil is smeared: vacuum first, use robot-safe cleaner, and pre-treat heavy oil.
92. Construction dust after renovation: do not use robot until large debris and fine cement dust are manually removed.
93. Outdoor balcony failure: use only enclosed dry balcony areas.
94. Toys and cords cause jams: clear floors before scheduled cleaning and use no-go zones.
95. Low furniture blocks access: set no-go zones or clean manually.

## Service Decisions
96. Do not self-repair internal electronics unless trained; sensors, seals, wiring, and batteries are easy to damage.
97. Contact official service for battery swelling, burning smell, repeated charging faults, motor overheating, or internal water damage.
98. Use original accessories when fit affects seals, brush rotation, mop pressure, or charging alignment.
99. After moving homes, inspect for physical damage, clean consumables, remap, and test docking.
100. Consider replacement if the robot is older than 3 years, has repeated motor/map/mop failures, and repair cost is high.
